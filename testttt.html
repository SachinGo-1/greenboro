Lead in Family and Community Support

Greensboro College’s online BA/BS in Human Development & Family Science (Interdisciplinary) draws from the fields of Education, Psychology, and Sociology, creating a well-rounded program that prepares students to support families and children outside traditional school settings. With career paths ranging from community organizer to center administrator and service professional, this program is ideal for students passionate about empowering families and giving children a voice in their communities to help them achieve their dreams and goals.

Key Features of Greensboro College’s BA/BS in Human Development & Family Science

Greensboro College’s online BA/BS in Human Development & Family Science provides a comprehensive, interdisciplinary education that prepares students to make a positive impact on children and families in diverse community settings.

Comprehensive Multidisciplinary Knowledge

Gain a well-rounded understanding of human development and family science by integrating key principles from psychology, sociology, and education. This broad perspective equips graduates to analyze and support child and family dynamics effectively.

Career-Focused Internship for Professional Readiness

Engage in a tailored internship aligned with your career goals, gaining practical experience in settings like childcare, early education, and social services. This hands-on experience builds confidence and prepares students for meaningful roles in the field.

Capstone Experience Demonstrating Expertise

Complete a capstone project that synthesizes knowledge across psychology, sociology, and education. This culminating experience demonstrates your readiness for professional roles or advanced academic study in human development and family science.

Individualized Support in Small Class Settings

With small class sizes, Greensboro College fosters an environment of personalized support. Faculty mentors provide guidance throughout your studies, ensuring a learning experience that aligns with your personal and professional aspirations.
